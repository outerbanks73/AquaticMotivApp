## 1. Executive Summary

**The core finding:** Aquatic Motiv's organic strength is concentrated in invertebrates — snails, clams, and crabs — where it holds top-3 positions against fragmented, low-authority competition. Its highest-volume plant terms, the category at the heart of the brand, are not ranking. Paid spend, not organic, is currently carrying plant demand.

**The opportunity is larger than plants.** A three-way competitor gap analysis revealed that the biggest territory being lost is not the contested plant head terms — it is an adjacent invertebrate and content space (shrimp, additional snail types, and a deep care-guide library) that a direct competitor is winning while Aquatic Motiv is absent. That space sits directly next to where Aquatic Motiv already wins, making it the highest-confidence area to expand.

**Technically the site is healthy.** The audit returned an alarming 1,265 "errors," but the overall health score is 88/100 and the site passes Core Web Vitals in the field on both mobile and desktop. The error count is inflated by template-level flags counted on every page; the genuinely actionable issues are a small, well-defined set.

### Top five priorities

1. **Extend the invertebrate moat.** Build snail/shrimp pages and guides for the adjacent terms a competitor already wins — bladder snails (10,800/mo), trumpet snails (6,600), cherry shrimp (27,100), neocaridina (14,800) — where Aquatic Motiv currently does not rank.
2. **Push five mid-page-1 snail terms into the top 3.** Nerite, mystery, and assassin snail terms already sit at positions 3–6 on pages that exist; content depth, internal links, and review schema can move them up, where click-through rises steeply.
3. **Win plants with content, not collection pages.** Both plant competitors rank through care guides and pillar content. Ship a flagship "Best Aquarium Plants" guide plus a species-page + care-guide layer, mirroring the snail-guide pattern that already works.
4. **Clear the real technical issues.** Repoint the global navigation links that redirect, surface two already-written beginner-plant blog posts that are orphaned, fix 18 broken high-ticket lighting images, and run a templated title/meta cleanup that doubles as keyword placement.
5. **Decide three merchandising questions.** Shrimp depth (on-thesis, high volume), water-lily/pond, and houseplant/terrarium are catalog decisions — not SEO tasks — that gate parts of the plan.

> **How to read this report.** Items are tagged by owner consistent with the operating model: **Vikram** makes strategic and merchandising decisions; **Francisco** executes implementation. The consolidated action plan in Section 6 carries owner and effort for every step.

### 2.3 Footprint notes

- **Traffic concentration.** The domain ranks for ~150+ keywords; a single term, "apt 3" (the 2Hr Aquarist APT 3 fertilizer product), is the largest single organic traffic source at position 19.
- **Volume caveat on "apt 3."** Its 110,000/mo volume is inflated by unrelated intent ("apartment 3") and the SERP is dominated by the brand itself. The realistic play is reseller intent ("APT 3 for sale," reviews), not the bare term.
- **AI-search presence is already an asset.** Aquatic Motiv holds position 1 in AI/SGE blocks for several care queries (liquid carbon, dwarf hairgrass, "do mystery snails eat algae"). Question-format care content is what wins these slots.
- **Engine variance.** A second Google config mirrors the primary engine; a third tracked engine shows materially weaker, more volatile positions. Blended "average position" roll-ups will therefore understate the primary-engine reality.

### 3.1 Two structurally different fronts

- **Inverts/snails — fragmented and winnable.** The only invert specialists are small (snailysnail.com, myhappysnails.com); none has the authority to own the head terms. This is why Aquatic Motiv sits top-3, and why the moat is expandable.
- **Plants — crowded with entrenched specialists.** aquariumplantsfactory, tropica, glassgrown, wetplants and others bring deep species catalogs plus years of care content. That incumbency is the wall on "aquarium plants" and "java fern."
- **Content / AI-Overviews — a defined target set.** aquariumcarebasics, keepfishkeeping and similar content sites win the informational SERPs and SGE slots. The pillar-guide strategy competes here directly.

> **Supplier exception — 2Hr Aquarist.** 2hraquarist.com is effectively a supplier (the APT brand) and dominates its own branded terms with ~56k traffic. Aquatic Motiv will not outrank the brand on its own name; the realistic target is reseller/buy-intent variants around its products.

## 5. Technical Audit Findings

**Crawl summary:** 1,246 pages crawled · health score 88/100 · 1,470 pages indexed by Google. Security, indexing, sitemap, and mobile checks are clean. Core Web Vitals pass in the field on both mobile and desktop (mobile: ~90% good LCP, 94% good CLS, 96% good INP).

> **Why the "1,265 errors" overstate the problem**
>
> - **extcss345xx (1,212):** one external CSS resource served via a redirect, counted once per page — a single Shopify theme/app asset, not 1,212 broken stylesheets. Cosmetic.
> - **internal links → 3XX (1,080):** the flagged pages are the homepage plus essentially every collection, i.e. a global nav/footer link (or a few) pointing to redirected handles. The fix is repointing a handful of template links, not 1,080 edits.
> - **JS/CSS not minified (1,212 each):** notices, not errors. Real optimization headroom, but not currently affecting Core Web Vitals.

### 5.1 Genuinely actionable issues

| Issue | Count | Severity | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Internal links → 3XX redirects | 1,080* | High | Equity/crawl waste; *template-scoped — few real link fixes |
| Titles too long | 419 | High | Truncated in SERPs; templated fix = keyword placement |
| Orphan pages (no inbound links) | 32** | High | **~12 real, incl. 2 beginner-plant blogs already written |
| Broken images (4XX) | 18 | High | Concentrated in high-ticket lighting SKUs; hurts conversion |
| Duplicate H1 | 218 | Medium | Weak heading signals across product/collection pages |
| Meta descriptions too long | 275 | Medium | Truncated snippets, lower CTR |
| Duplicate meta descriptions | 193 | Medium | Templated fix |
| Titles too short | 50 | Medium | Wasted title real estate |
| Missing image alt text | 28 | Medium | Image SEO + accessibility + AI legibility |
| Missing meta description | 19 | Medium | — |
| Missing H1 | 14 | Medium | — |

### 5.2 Specific findings worth acting on now

- **Buried beginner content.** Two already-written posts — "top-10-easy-aquarium-plants-for-beginners" and "top-5-best-foreground-carpet-plants-for-beginners" — are orphaned with zero internal links. This is exactly the beginner-plant content the strategy calls for; it simply needs surfacing.
- **Important orphaned collections.** /collections/inverts, /snail-food, /sulcospira, /lobsters-crayfish, /water-lettuce, /terrariums and /wysiwyg-bettas have no inbound internal links. (Ignorable orphans: agents.md, vendors?q= filters, theme home-page.)
- **Broken lighting images.** The 18 broken images are almost all Week Aqua / Ledstar / Hygger product pages and lighting blog posts — high-ticket items where a missing image directly costs conversions.
- **Cannibalization.** "mystery snail" (22,200) ranks at both a collection (pos 7) and a product page (pos 16); consolidate signals to the collection.
